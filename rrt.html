Rational Root Theorem

The Rational Root Theorem states that the possible roots of polynomial function is in the form p/q, where p is the factors of the constant, and q is the factors of the leading coefficient.

Example

Because the constant is -8 and the leading coefficient is 4, the factors of -8 will be the numerator and the factors of 4 will be the denominator

-8: 1, 2, 4, 8

4: 1, 2, 4

The possible roots of the polynomial is:

1/4, 1/2, 1, 2, 4, 8

(All of these could be positive or negative)

If you want to find the actual roots, you'd have to plug in the possible roots
